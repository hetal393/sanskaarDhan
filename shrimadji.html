Shrimad Rajchandraji

"જેણે આત્મા જાણ્યો, તેણે સર્વ જાણ્યો"

Param KrupaluDev

His full name was Shri Raichandbhai Ravjibhai Mehta. He was born in 1867 A.D. at Vavania in Saurashtra. His family belonged to a well-known merchant community. His father's name was Ravjibhai and mother's name was Devabai. His mother was brought up in Jain religious traditions. His grand father was a staunch devotee of Lord Krishna.

Dawn of a New Era

1867

On the radiant full moon night of Kartik Purnima, V.S. 1924 (9 November, 1867), the port town of Vavania, Gujarat, was blessed with the arrival of Raichand. Little did His parents, Devba and Ravjibhai Mehta, know that the infant they held in their hands, would hold the hands of multitude of seekers, guiding them to eternal liberation.

The Awakening

1875

At a tender age of 7 years, with an anxiety to know the meaning of Death, Shrimad climbed the Banayan Tree and quietly saw the cremation Process. On seeing an unusual thing of burning a person, Shrimad went into deep thinking and realized his past lives (jatismaran-gyan) as well as he being the last Disciple of Lord Mahaveer.

Bal Yogi

1875 - 1884

At his first day in the school, he knew everything whatever the Teacher taught, his teaching skills were so powerful that the teacher would sit beside and the Child Shrimad thaught the class. Child Shrimad would explain verses of Ramayan/ Mahabharata to intellectual scholars. Besides his intelligence, he also possessed qualities like Mercy, Gratitude, Honesty. He was popularly known "Kavi" for his poetic writings and philosophies. At an age of 13, Shrimad started investigating the true religion. In an year’s time, he finished reading nearly all the major religions and then concluded that "Vitraag Dharam" is the true religion.

Performing Avdhaan

1887

Avdhan means one can remember and perform many tasks simultaneously, without error. It is difficult to perform even five tasks at a time for an ordinary human,whereas Shrimad gave a public demonstration of shatavdhan (performing one hundred tasks simultaneously) at Sir Framji Cawasji Institute on 22 January, 1887 in Mumbai, at an age of nineteen. But when he realized that the fame is causing distraction on his spiritual progress, he declined to perform any other avdhaan in his life. Shrimad married Zabakben, daughter of Popatlal, the elder brother of Revashankar Jagjivandas Mehta, a Zaveri merchant family. He then engaged in the pearls and diamond business.

Spiritual Work by Shrimad

1890

Rajchandra wrote Stri Niti Bodhaka (The Nature of Ideal Moral Life for Women) in which he had advocated women's education as essential to national freedom. Sad-bodh-shatak is his work on ethical topics. Mokshamala is about Jainism and self-liberation written in an easy style understandable to young people. In 1890 (V.S. 1947), he experienced the self-realization(Samyak Darshan) for the first time at Uttarsanda where he was meditating under a mango tree near a lake.

Detachment from worldly pleasures

1890 - 1900

On attaining samkit, shrimad stayed in Gujarat with his disciples. He retired from householder life and business when he was thirty. He spent months in the hills of Idar and the forests of Kavitha, Vaso, Uttarsanda, and Kheda where he had instructed seven monks in religious discourses sitting on a stone, pudhvi śila. On Aso Vad Ekam in 1896, in Nadiad, He composed the glorious 142 verses of Shri Atmasiddhi Shastra. He wrote more than 900 letters which charts his spiritual journey and teachings to disciples. His several poems are popular including "Apoorva Avsar Evo Kyare Aavshe..", "Mool Marg Sambhlo Jinno Re..", "Bina Nayan Pavey Nahi..", "Hey Prabhu! Hey Prabhu! Shu Kahu..", "Yam Niyam Sanjam Aap Kiyo..", "Ichche Chhe Je Jogijan..".

Shrimad in his Last Days

1901

He contracted an illness during his stay in Dharampur, Gujarat, from which he never recovered. He died on 9 April 1901 (Chaitra Vad 5, V.S. 1957) in Rajkot. He inspired many monks, philosophers, spiritual seekers and scholars, out of which 4 great people attained "Atmagyaan". One of them was Param Pujya Laghuraj Swami.
